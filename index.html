About Me

Today is the first day of the rest of your life, grab the bull by the horns and go!

If I could be anywhere, it would be the mountains. That is where I feel most at peace with the world. When I'm not in the mountains or bull fighting, I live in Henrico, Virginia. Henrico is a county that surrounds the state capital, Richmond. I am originally from Western Massachusetts, did some time in New Jersey and Williamsburg, Virginia before landing in the Richmond area in 1990.

One of my current hobbies is pursuing state high points. Each state has a highest point, if only a rise on the side of the road. Alaska boasts the highest, Denali/Mount McKinley at 20,146 ft above sea level. Florida has the lowest highpoint at 345 ft. I have currently completed 29, with more on the calendar for this year.

I have spent the entirety of my professional life in the software development arena. I received my associate's degree with a focus on programming and business at the time when personal computers were just making the scene. I was fortunate to have the opportunity to by-pass mainframes and get in on the cutting edge technologies. I started my career at the University of Massachusetts, and then went on to work in various industries including; insurance, mortgage banking, custom contract development, consulting and the pharmaceutical industry. During that time, I returned to school to complete my Bachelor's degree in Information Systems at the University of Richmond.

Coming full-circle, I have just recently taken a position in the Administrative Services department at my alma mater. My first assignment required development in Oracle APEX (Application Express), a language I didn't know. I have spent the first four months learning the technology, and have my first project ready for deployment. One thing I discovered is that I need a more in-depth understanding of the web development stack, particularly HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. To accomplish this, I have enrolled in an intensive 6-month boot camp. I have been in and out of management positions, but found I prefer the hands on technical an analytical components of development. I especially enjoy meeting with the business teams to understand how technology can solve some of their challenges.
